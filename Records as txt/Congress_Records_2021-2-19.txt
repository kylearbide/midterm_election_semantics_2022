Committee Meetings 
(Committees not listed did not meet) 
CORONAVIRUS RECOVERY 
Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs: On 
Thursday, February 18, 2021, Committee concluded 
a hearing to examine the Coronavirus crisis, focusing 
on paving the way to an equitable recovery, after re-
ceiving testimony from Mayor Jamael Tito Brown, 
Youngstown, Ohio; John A. Costa, Amalgamated 
Transit Union, Silver Spring, Maryland; Jyoshu 
Tsushima, Legal Aid Society of Columbus, Colum-
bus, Ohio; Douglas Holtz-Eakin, American Action 
Forum, Washington, D.C.; and John Cracchiolo, Sal 
and Jerry’s Bakery, West Babylon, New York.

House of Representatives 
Chamber Action 
The House was not in session today. The House 
is scheduled to meet in Pro Forma session at 11:30 
a.m. on Monday, February 22, 2021. 
Committee Meetings 
COVID–19 AND THE CHILD CARE CRISIS 
Committee on Appropriations: Subcommittee on the De-
partments of Labor, Health and Human Services, 
Education, and Related Agencies held a hearing en-
titled ‘‘COVID–19 and the Child Care Crisis’’. Tes-
timony was heard from Theresa Hawley, First Assist-
ant Deputy Governor, Education, Office of Governor, 
Illinois; and public witnesses. 
DEPARTMENT OF VETERANS AFFAIRS 
COVID–19 RESPONSE 
Committee on Appropriations: Subcommittee on Mili-
tary Construction, Veterans Affairs, and Related 
Agencies held a hearing entitled ‘‘Department of 
Veterans Affairs COVID–19 Response’’. Testimony 
was heard from Richard Stone, Acting Under Sec-
retary for Health, Veterans Health Administration, 
Department of Veterans Affairs. 
MISCELLANEOUS MEASURES 
Committee on House Administration: Full Committee 
held a business meeting on Committee Resolutions 
to establish procedures in contested election cases 
properly filed under the Federal Contested Election 
Act, and related matters. Committee Resolutions to 
establish procedures in contested election cases prop-
erly filed under the Federal Contested Election Act, 
and related matters were adopted. 
A PATHWAY FOR PEACE IN 
AFGHANISTAN: EXAMINING THE 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS OF 
THE AFGHANISTAN STUDY GROUP 
Committee on Oversight and Reform: Subcommittee on 
National Security held a hearing entitled ‘‘A Path-
way for Peace in Afghanistan: Examining the Find-
ings and Recommendations of the Afghanistan Study 
Group’’. Testimony was heard from the following 
Afghanistan Study Group officials: Kelly A. Ayotte, 
Co-Chair; General Joseph F. Dunford, Jr. (Retired), 
Co-Chair; and Nancy Lindborg, Co-Chair.
